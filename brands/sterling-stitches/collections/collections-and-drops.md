# Sterling Stitches — Collections and Drops

> This register captures every grouped product release Sterling Stitches has planned, launched, or intends to launch. It is the supply-side map that drives editorial weight, launch timing, and campaign sequencing. Update before each planning cycle.

---

### Vals Day Collection

- **Launch Period:** Early February, targeting February 7–14 annually
- **Products Included:** Black Velvet Agbada, Black/Red Aso-oke Trouser, White Native Shirt, Aso-oke Yoruba Cap, Sterling Vintage Floral Shirt
- **Central Thesis:** Valentine's Day in Nigeria is not a soft holiday — it is a statement. This collection gives the man who wants to be remembered something to arrive in: pieces that declare intention without announcing price. Every item in the Vals Day Collection is chosen for how it photographs and how it makes the wearer feel when he walks through a door.
- **Campaign Importance:** anchor
- **Limited or Evergreen:** seasonal
- **Launch Type:** capsule
- **Supporting Notes:** One of the brand's two biggest annual commercial windows alongside Christmas. Editorial lead time should begin January 20. Content posture is celebratory and aspirational — avoid generic "be her Valentine" tropes; position around the man's confidence and presence. Valentine's Day is February 14; editorial should peak 7–10 days before. Cross-reference with the Vals Day occasion entry in occasions-and-celebrations.md.

### Ramadan Drop

- **Launch Period:** First week of Ramadan (date shifts annually with lunar calendar; approximately late February to early April)
- **Products Included:** White Native Shirt, Black/Red Aso-oke Trouser, Aso-oke Yoruba Cap, Black Velvet Agbada
- **Central Thesis:** Ramadan is a month of discipline, community, and considered appearance. The Ramadan Drop brings together pieces that carry the weight of the occasion — garments appropriate for prayers, Iftar gatherings, and the quiet elevated dressing that marks the month. Each piece is chosen for modesty, cultural resonance, and the kind of presence that honors the moment.
- **Campaign Importance:** major
- **Limited or Evergreen:** seasonal
- **Launch Type:** capsule
- **Supporting Notes:** Audience is primarily Muslim men in Nigeria and diaspora. Editorial posture must be editorial-led or community-led — commerce should be embedded with cultural sensitivity. Avoid sale framing. Tone: reverent, generous, unhurried. Eid al-Fitr follows at the end of Ramadan — plan a post-Ramadan Eid-focused editorial continuation. Dates shift each year; confirm lunar calendar before committing to launch window.

### Easter Drop

- **Launch Period:** Holy Week, targeting the week before Good Friday (date shifts annually; typically late March to late April)
- **Products Included:** Sunset Dashiki Trousers, White Native Shirt, Sterling Vintage Floral Shirt, Sterling Vintage Shirt — Gold Abstract Art, The Purple Ankara Trousers
- **Central Thesis:** Easter in Nigeria means colour, reunion, and the best outfit you've worn all year. The Easter Drop is built around the church-to-celebration arc: garments that carry through from morning service to the afternoon gathering, from formal to festive. Everything is chosen for brightness, confidence, and the specific social visibility of Easter Sunday.
- **Campaign Importance:** major
- **Limited or Evergreen:** seasonal
- **Launch Type:** full collection
- **Supporting Notes:** Easter is a significant social dressing occasion for Christian Nigerians. Editorial tone: celebratory, bright, culturally warm. Good Friday falls before Easter Sunday — editorial can acknowledge the full arc. Launch content 10–14 days before Good Friday. Cross-reference with Easter and Good Friday entries in occasions-and-celebrations.md.

### Back to School Collection

- **Launch Period:** Late August, targeting August 20 — September 5 annually
- **Products Included:** Sterling Vintage Shirt — Gold Abstract Art, Sterling Vintage Floral Shirt, Black Adire Trousers With Striped Colored Pockets, The Purple Ankara Trousers
- **Central Thesis:** Back to school at university in Nigeria is a social event as much as an academic one. The Back to School Collection is for the young man who arrives on campus knowing that how he dresses sends a message before he says a word. These pieces are contemporary, culturally grounded, and ready to move between lecture halls, social gatherings, and the street.
- **Campaign Importance:** standard
- **Limited or Evergreen:** seasonal
- **Launch Type:** capsule
- **Supporting Notes:** Primary audience is university students and young professionals in their early twenties. Secondary audience: parents buying for sons. Tone: confident, modern, socially aware. Distinguish Sterling Stitches from generic youth fashion — lean into cultural identity as aspiration, not restriction. Nigerian universities resume September; editorial should peak the last two weeks of August.

### Labour Day Drop

- **Launch Period:** Last week of April, targeting launch on or before May 1 (Workers' Day / Labour Day, Nigeria)
- **Products Included:** Sunset Dashiki Trousers, Red Adire Trousers, Black Adire Trousers With Striped Colored Pockets, White Native Shirt
- **Central Thesis:** May 1 belongs to the worker — the man who shows up, builds, and creates. The Labour Day Drop is a quiet declaration: this is what the working man wears when he chooses to be seen as himself, not as his role. Artisan-made, cultural in origin, and designed for a day when dignity is the only dress code.
- **Campaign Importance:** standard
- **Limited or Evergreen:** seasonal
- **Launch Type:** capsule
- **Supporting Notes:** Workers' Day is a national public holiday in Nigeria. Editorial angle: celebrate labor, craft, and the artisan hands behind Sterling Stitches products. This collection is naturally suited to a Craft Study or Manifesto latazin type that connects the garment to the people who make it. Tone: civic, warm, grounded. Avoid generic "workers deserve a treat" framing.

### Christmas Collection

- **Launch Period:** December 1–20 annually (pre-Christmas delivery window)
- **Products Included:** Black Velvet Agbada, Black/Red Aso-oke Trouser, White Native Shirt, Aso-oke Yoruba Cap, Sunset Dashiki Trousers, Red Adire Trousers
- **Central Thesis:** Christmas in Nigeria is one of the year's great dressing occasions. The Christmas Collection is the brand's most complete seasonal statement: pieces for the church service, the family compound gathering, and the celebration that stretches through Boxing Day into the new year. This collection carries the weight of the season — it is not a gift guide, it is a wardrobe for the most watched weeks of the year.
- **Campaign Importance:** anchor
- **Limited or Evergreen:** seasonal
- **Launch Type:** full collection
- **Supporting Notes:** This is the brand's highest-volume commercial window alongside Vals Day. Editorial launch should begin late November; peak editorial week is December 8–15 to catch pre-Christmas purchasing. Tone: festive, generous, elevated. Christmas in Abuja and Lagos is a major social occasion — editorial should reflect the specific visual and cultural texture of a Nigerian Christmas. Cross-reference with Christmas Day and Boxing Day entries in occasions-and-celebrations.md.

### Sterling Vintage Series

- **Launch Period:** Rolling — pieces added throughout the year
- **Products Included:** Sterling Vintage Shirt — Gold Abstract Art, Sterling Vintage Floral Shirt
- **Central Thesis:** The Sterling Vintage Series is the brand's ongoing exploration of what contemporary African menswear looks like when it draws from the continent's visual vocabulary without trying to be a museum piece. Each shirt in the series is a distinct design object — made to be worn, not explained.
- **Campaign Importance:** minor
- **Limited or Evergreen:** evergreen
- **Launch Type:** expansion
- **Supporting Notes:** Individual pieces in this series can be featured within other collections or as standalone Product Argument latazins. Strong editorial potential for Craft Study or Misreading Correction latazins exploring what "African print" means beyond the obvious.

- The six named collections (Vals Day, Ramadan Drop, Easter Drop, Back to School, Labour Day Drop, Christmas) are the brand's primary seasonal events. All six should appear in the annual editorial calendar.
- Vals Day and Christmas are anchor campaigns; all others are major or standard.
- Ramadan Drop dates shift every year — confirm lunar calendar dates at the start of each annual planning cycle.
- Easter Drop dates also shift — confirm liturgical calendar dates at the start of each annual planning cycle.
