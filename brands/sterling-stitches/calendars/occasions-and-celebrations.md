# Sterling Stitches — Occasions and Celebrations

> This register captures every occasion, celebration, holiday, and cultural moment relevant to Sterling Stitches and its Nigerian audience. It includes national public holidays, religious observances, monthly new-month celebrations, and brand collection anchors. Dates for Islamic holidays are approximate and shift annually with the lunar calendar — confirm dates before each planning cycle.

---

## Monthly New-Month Celebrations

### New Month — January

- **Date or Period:** 1 January
- **Region Relevance:** Nigeria; global
- **Tone:** hopeful, ambitious, fresh
- **Editorial Posture:** editorial-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Calendar Marking, Manifesto, Product Argument
- **Product Fit:** pieces that frame a new year with intention; year-round wardrobe investments; Agbada for those who start the year at a ceremony
- **Supporting Notes:** New Year's Day is a public holiday. The new-month moment coincides with the year's biggest reset. Tone: forward-looking, specific to the man Sterling Stitches dresses — not generic "new year new you" language. Editorial framing: what does it mean to start the year dressed deliberately?

### New Month — February

- **Date or Period:** 1 February
- **Region Relevance:** Nigeria
- **Tone:** charged, intimate, anticipatory
- **Editorial Posture:** editorial-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Calendar Marking, Bundle/World

- **Supporting Notes:** February opens with Valentine's season already in the air. The new-month marker should signal the Vals Day Collection arrival. Cross-reference Vals Day entry below.

### New Month — March

- **Supporting Notes:** Ramadan frequently starts in late February or early March. The new-month moment aligns naturally with a Ramadan Drop editorial opening. Tone: respectful, unhurried. Cross-reference Ramadan entry.

### New Month — April

## Nigerian National Public Holidays

### New Year's Day

- **Date or Period:** 1 January
- **Region Relevance:** Nigeria; global
- **Tone:** hopeful, fresh, forward
- **Editorial Posture:** editorial-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Calendar Marking, Manifesto
- **Product Fit:** year-round investment pieces; hero garments for a new year
- **Supporting Notes:** Coincides with January new-month celebration. See New Month — January entry for combined editorial guidance.

### Workers' Day (Labour Day)

- **Date or Period:** 1 May
- **Region Relevance:** Nigeria; global
- **Tone:** civic, dignified, grounded
- **Editorial Posture:** editorial-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Occasion, Manifesto, Craft Study, Calendar Marking
- **Product Fit:** Labour Day Drop — Adire trousers, Dashiki, White Native Shirt; artisan-made products with provenance story
- **Supporting Notes:** Coincides with the Labour Day Drop launch and the May new-month celebration. This triple overlap makes May 1 one of the brand's most editorially rich single-day moments. Craft Study latazin connecting the Adire dyeing process to the occasion is a strong editorial choice.

### Democracy Day

- **Date or Period:** 12 June
- **Region Relevance:** Nigeria
- **Tone:** civic, proud, reflective
- **Editorial Posture:** editorial-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Calendar Marking, Manifesto
- **Product Fit:** culturally significant pieces; Aso-oke and Agbada as expressions of Nigerian identity
- **Supporting Notes:** Celebrates Nigeria's return to civilian democratic governance. Not a major commerce window but an important cultural moment. Editorial angle: the Nigerian man as a citizen who dresses with intention — not just for occasions but as a daily assertion of identity.

### Independence Day

- **Date or Period:** 1 October
- **Region Relevance:** Nigeria; global Nigerian diaspora
- **Tone:** proud, celebratory, civic
- **Editorial Posture:** editorial-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Manifesto, Calendar Marking, Occasion, Craft Study
- **Product Fit:** Aso-oke products, Black Velvet Agbada, Aso-oke Yoruba Cap, White Native Shirt — pieces with explicit Yoruba-Nigerian cultural heritage
- **Supporting Notes:** Nigeria's most significant national holiday. Coincides with October new-month celebration. For Sterling Stitches, this is a foundational editorial moment: a brand built on Yoruba textile heritage celebrating Nigerian independence. Tone must be authoritative and culturally specific — not generic patriotism. The brand has earned the right to speak to this occasion.

### Christmas Day

- **Date or Period:** 25 December
- **Region Relevance:** Nigeria; diaspora
- **Tone:** festive, generous, warm
- **Editorial Posture:** commerce-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Occasion, Bundle/World, Collection/Drop, Shop the Look
- **Product Fit:** Christmas Collection in full; Black Velvet Agbada (signature statement piece); complete occasion ensembles
- **Supporting Notes:** One of the brand's two anchor commercial events. Christmas in Nigeria involves church services, family gatherings at home, and social visibility throughout the day. The brand's editorial approach should reflect this full social arc — morning through evening — not just a generic festive moment.

### Boxing Day

- **Date or Period:** 26 December
- **Region Relevance:** Nigeria; Commonwealth countries
- **Tone:** relaxed, generous, continued celebration
- **Editorial Posture:** editorial-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Calendar Marking, Bundle/World
- **Product Fit:** relaxed occasion wear; Vintage shirts for Boxing Day gatherings; pieces that extend Christmas dressing into the day after
- **Supporting Notes:** In Nigeria, Boxing Day is often as social as Christmas Day — more informal gatherings, visits, and extended family time. Editorial: the morning after Christmas, dressed with the same intention but less formality.

## Religious Observances

### Valentine's Day

- **Date or Period:** 14 February
- **Region Relevance:** Nigeria; global
- **Tone:** romantic, celebratory, aspirational
- **Editorial Posture:** commerce-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Occasion, Bundle/World, Shop the Look, Collection/Drop
- **Product Fit:** Vals Day Collection — Black Velvet Agbada, Black/Red Aso-oke Trouser, White Native Shirt, Aso-oke Yoruba Cap, Sterling Vintage Floral Shirt
- **Supporting Notes:** Valentine's Day is a major commercial and social occasion in Nigerian cities. Tone: confident, celebratory, aspirational — frame around the man who arrives with presence and intention. Lead editorial 10–14 days before February 14. Cross-reference Vals Day Collection entry in collections-and-drops.md.

### Ramadan

- **Date or Period:** Variable — follows lunar calendar; approximately late February to early April (shifts earlier each year by approximately 11 days)
- **Region Relevance:** Muslim communities in Nigeria; global Muslim diaspora
- **Tone:** reverent, disciplined, community-oriented
- **Editorial Posture:** editorial-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Calendar Marking, Occasion, Bundle/World
- **Product Fit:** Ramadan Drop — White Native Shirt, Black/Red Aso-oke Trouser, Aso-oke Yoruba Cap, Black Velvet Agbada
- **Supporting Notes:** Nigeria has a large Muslim population, especially in the north and among Yoruba communities. The Ramadan Drop requires culturally sensitive, non-commercial editorial posture. Avoid sale or discount framing. Focus on appropriate dressing for the month: prayers, Iftar gatherings, and the considered appearance that marks the holy month.

### Good Friday

- **Date or Period:** Friday before Easter Sunday (date shifts annually; typically late March to late April)
- **Region Relevance:** Christian communities in Nigeria; global
- **Tone:** solemn, reflective
- **Editorial Posture:** observe only
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** none — internal calendar awareness only
- **Product Fit:** none — editorial only
- **Supporting Notes:** Good Friday is a national public holiday in Nigeria. Sterling Stitches should not publish commerce content on Good Friday. Use the day as an editorial pause; any Easter Drop content should publish the days before or after.

### Easter Sunday / Easter Monday

- **Date or Period:** Sunday and Monday following Good Friday (date shifts annually)
- **Region Relevance:** Christian communities in Nigeria; global
- **Tone:** celebratory, joyful, social
- **Editorial Posture:** commerce-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Occasion, Collection/Drop, Bundle/World, Shop the Look
- **Product Fit:** Easter Drop — Sunset Dashiki Trousers, White Native Shirt, Sterling Vintage Floral Shirt, Sterling Vintage Shirt — Gold Abstract Art, The Purple Ankara Trousers
- **Supporting Notes:** Easter is a major dressing occasion for Christian Nigerians — church on Sunday morning, family gatherings in the afternoon, social visibility throughout. The Easter Drop should be live by Palm Sunday. Cross-reference Easter Drop entry in collections-and-drops.md.

### Eid al-Fitr

- **Date or Period:** Variable — follows lunar calendar; marks the end of Ramadan (approximately late March to early May, shifts annually)
- **Region Relevance:** Muslim communities in Nigeria; global Muslim diaspora
- **Tone:** generous, celebratory, joyful
- **Editorial Posture:** editorial-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Occasion, Bundle/World, Calendar Marking
- **Product Fit:** Black Velvet Agbada, Aso-oke trousers, White Native Shirt, Aso-oke Yoruba Cap — complete formal occasion ensembles
- **Supporting Notes:** Eid al-Fitr is one of the most significant dressing occasions of the year for Muslim men in Nigeria. New garments are traditional for Eid. Editorial tone: generous, celebratory, culturally warm. Commerce is appropriate here if the posture remains editorial-led. Publish Eid content the week before the anticipated date.

### Eid al-Adha (Eid el-Kabir)

- **Date or Period:** Variable — follows lunar calendar; approximately June to July (shifts annually)
- **Region Relevance:** Muslim communities in Nigeria; global Muslim diaspora
- **Tone:** reverent, generous, communal
- **Editorial Posture:** editorial-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Occasion, Bundle/World, Calendar Marking
- **Product Fit:** Black Velvet Agbada, Aso-oke trousers, White Native Shirt, Aso-oke Yoruba Cap
- **Supporting Notes:** Eid al-Adha (also called Eid el-Kabir in Nigeria) is observed with prayers, the sharing of meat, and communal gatherings. Dressing is formal and occasion-significant. Editorial posture mirrors Eid al-Fitr — culturally respectful, community-centered, with commerce embedded gently.

### Eid-el-Maulud (Prophet's Birthday)

- **Date or Period:** Variable — follows lunar calendar; approximately September (shifts annually)
- **Region Relevance:** Muslim communities in Nigeria
- **Tone:** reverent, communal
- **Editorial Posture:** observe only
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Calendar Marking (optional, minimal)
- **Product Fit:** none — editorial only
- **Supporting Notes:** A national public holiday in Nigeria. Sterling Stitches may acknowledge the occasion with a Calendar Marking post if the brand serves this audience credibly, but should not attach commerce. Optional observance.

## Cultural and Civic Moments

### Children's Day

- **Date or Period:** 27 May
- **Region Relevance:** Nigeria
- **Tone:** warm, familial, joyful
- **Editorial Posture:** community-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Calendar Marking
- **Product Fit:** none — editorial only; or gifting pieces for boys if relevant
- **Supporting Notes:** A national celebration in Nigeria for children. For Sterling Stitches (menswear brand), this is primarily an acknowledgment moment. Optional: a latazin about the next generation of men who will wear the brand — cultural continuity angle.

### Father's Day

- **Date or Period:** Third Sunday in June
- **Region Relevance:** Nigeria; global
- **Tone:** warm, respectful, celebratory
- **Editorial Posture:** commerce-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Occasion, Bundle/World, Shop the Look
- **Product Fit:** high-giftability products — Black Velvet Agbada, Aso-oke Yoruba Cap, White Native Shirt; pieces the Intentional Gifter would select for a father
- **Supporting Notes:** Father's Day is a growing occasion in Nigerian social culture. Strong gifting moment for Sterling Stitches — many of the brand's formal pieces are natural Father's Day gifts. Editorial lead time: 7–10 days before.

### Owambe Season (Nigerian Social Events)

- **Date or Period:** Year-round; peaks in March–April, July–August, and December
- **Region Relevance:** Nigeria; Yoruba communities globally
- **Tone:** celebratory, social, vibrant
- **Editorial Posture:** editorial-led
- **Suitable Latazin Types:** Bundle/World, Shop the Look, Occasion
- **Product Fit:** Black Velvet Agbada, Aso-oke trousers, Aso-oke Yoruba Cap, Dashiki — complete occasion ensembles; the full Sterling Stitches product range
- **Supporting Notes:** Owambe is the Nigerian term for large, celebratory social events — weddings, naming ceremonies, anniversaries, and parties. This is the primary cultural context for formal African menswear in Nigeria. Owambe season does not have a single date but represents a recurring editorial opportunity year-round. Sterling Stitches should have a standing Owambe editorial angle in its content library.

## Usage Notes

- Lunar calendar dates for Ramadan, Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, and Eid-el-Maulud shift by approximately 11 days earlier each year. Confirm exact dates from the Islamic calendar at the start of each annual planning cycle.
- Easter dates also shift annually — confirm from the liturgical calendar.
- The new-month entries above represent a standing editorial commitment: every first of the month is an occasion to celebrate the new month in Sterling Stitches voice.
- Cross-reference all occasion product fit fields with `product-catalogue.md` and all collection references with `collections-and-drops.md`.
- When multiple occasions cluster (e.g., May 1 as both Workers' Day, Labour Day Drop launch, and new-month marker), consolidate into a single editorial moment rather than three separate posts.
