# Reference Library

## Purpose

This is a curated library of external editorial and commerce references — publications, brands, campaigns, and stylistic benchmarks that demonstrate the editorial standards the Latazin system aspires to. It exists to calibrate taste, tone, and structural decisions across teams and over time.

References are not models to copy. They are examples that illuminate *what good looks like* in specific dimensions: voice, structure, product presentation, visual identity, pacing, or cultural positioning. Each entry explains what to learn from the reference and why it matters to the system.

---

## How This File Is Used in the System

Consult this library when:

- **Defining house voice** — to find examples of the tonal register the brand wants to inhabit or avoid.
- **Briefing a latazin** — to point a writer or generator toward structural or stylistic precedents.
- **Reviewing content quality** — to compare output against external benchmarks and calibrate editorial judgment.
- **Onboarding new team members** — to quickly communicate the editorial standard through concrete examples.
- **Planning campaigns** — to study how other brands or publications have structured editorial commerce.

```
Reference Library (this file)
         │
         ├──▶ House Voice (tonal calibration)
         ├──▶ Latazin Briefs (structural and stylistic direction)
         ├──▶ Quality Reviews (benchmark comparison)
         └──▶ Team Onboarding (standard-setting)
```

## How to Add New References

When you encounter a publication, brand, campaign, or stylistic example worth preserving, add it here using the reference entry format above. Follow these guidelines:

1. **Be specific about the lesson.** "This brand is cool" is not a reference. "This brand demonstrates how to write about leather goods with material specificity and without adjective stacking" is a reference.
2. **Categorise accurately.** Place the entry under the most appropriate heading. If it fits multiple categories, choose the primary one.
3. **Explain relevance to the system.** Connect the reference to specific parts of the Latazin system — a latazin type, a page role, a voice characteristic, a structural pattern.
4. **Include caveats.** Note what *not* to take from the reference if applicable. A brand may have excellent visual identity but weak editorial voice — say so.
5. **Keep it current.** Remove or archive references that are no longer accessible, no longer relevant, or no longer represent the standard the team aspires to.
6. **Quality over quantity.** A library of 15 well-documented references is more useful than 50 entries with vague descriptions. Add only references that genuinely inform editorial decisions.
